# Appendix G — Energy Consumption and Thermal Management

The artificial kidney operates within a 50–200 mW electrical envelope. All
electrical work dissipates as heat, requiring a defined thermal pathway to
maintain protein stability and hemodynamic safety.

Heat is conducted through the device shell into surrounding tissue and removed
primarily by local blood perfusion. Under expected loads, modeled steady-state
temperature rise remains within 1–2 °C, below thresholds affecting plasma
proteins or coagulation.

Thermal management is treated as a –3 stabilization offset buffering the
dissipative load generated by +2. The 0-layer continuity ensures uniform heat
distribution, while ±∞ boundaries maintain immune isolation without trapping
heat.
